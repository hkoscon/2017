Talk

Portfolio Management through storytelling using narrative visualizations with advanced filtering: Crossfilter + d3.js = dc.js

Heena Mahour

Visualizations act as a campfire around which we gather to tell stories (AI Shalloway, 2011). Most sophisticated visualization tools supports visual encodings and analysis routines but lacks interactivity to support narrative communication. Hence, it diminishes the expressiveness,i.e, the diversity of possible visualizations and leads to substantial runtime overhead. It remains an open question that how these tools be upgraded to support optimal and wide range of storytelling. This talk deals with the amalgamation of narrative storytelling with interactive and responsive visualizations through advanced filtering.

According to Ben Shneiderman(2003), a picture is worth a thousand words. An interface is worth a thousand pictures. Hence, with the rise in visualization techniques, the need to catalogue and consolidate it arises as well. In recent years, static visualizations via diagrams and charts are used for author driven financial storytelling in which the text provides the outline and the image depicts the related details. Prior to that, conventional raw dataset was used to provide details to the subject. Though it clarifies the visual elements but produces clutter.According to a recent survey, less than 25% of the individuals over the age of 50 were not aware of fundamental economic concepts such as exposure, inflation and hence failed to invest properly. It refers to the need of proper financial planning which is inculcated with the use of narrative communication through visualization techniques with runtime filtering so that the visualization could be manipulated by the users. Through prior research work on drill down stories, the author observed the need for single frame interactivity and consistent visual platform for reader driven stories.

Thereby, enabling the end users to foster the decision making by visualizing aggregated risk and other factors efficiently. This talk thus investigates a problem statement to visualize reader driven top performers involving exposure and profit allocation across a varied set of dimensions such as funds, segment and sector to illustrate design space using graphical techniques and interactivity enforcing a narrative flow. Later, the talk also illustrates a narrative exploratory analysis of high dimensional datasets using brushing technique.
